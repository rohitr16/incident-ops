Dashboard Layout Direction

Select the design direction that best fits the POC requirements.

A

Split-Screen Control Center (Recommended)

A dual-pane layout built for active monitoring and triage. The left pane hosts a live list of incident cards; clicking a card loads it into the right-pane inspector, which displays raw logs, classification metadata, and matching runbook actions.

B

Metrics & Grid Dashboard

A grid-based dashboard focused on overall system health. Displays high-level KPI cards at the top (Active Incidents, MTTResolve, SLA compliance, etc.), followed by a grid of incident cards grouped by category or severity. Click to open details in a modal.

C

Unified Centered Feed

A clean, single-column feed showing incidents as a chronological timeline. Each incident card expands inline with smooth accordion transitions to reveal matching resolution actions and triage logic. Minimalist and focused.

Pros & Cons Comparison

Option A: Split-Screen

Pros: Excellent for deep inspection; keeps log context visible while executing playbooks; matches real-world NOC/ops tools.

Cons: Requires more desktop screen space; can look complex on small screens.

Option B: Grid & Metrics

Pros: Immediate overview of metrics; clear visual cues on volume of issues; great for executives/managers.

Cons: Triage and playbook actions are hidden behind modals, adding extra clicks.
